Welcome to Driochta

Driochta is a land steeped in history and mystery, where ancient magic has recently begun to reawaken after centuries of dormancy. Divided into four distinct provinces—each governed by a noble house—Driochta is a realm of diverse landscapes, from the fertile plains of Errah to the towering peaks of Tronjem. While peace currently reigns within its borders, the return of magic has stirred strange occurrences and uncovered forgotten secrets, setting the stage for a new era of adventure. As you journey through this enchanting land, prepare to encounter powerful deities, hidden factions, and a world where the line between myth and reality blurs.

The land

Errah

Errah, the northernmost province, is the agricultural heart of Driochta, known for its vast, fertile plains and skilled artisans. The province is dotted with small villages and farms, where the majority of the country’s food and raw materials are produced. A serene and peaceful region, Errah is characterized by rolling grasslands, dense forests, and a small desert to the far north. Its people are hardworking and self-sufficient, with a deep connection to the land and its rhythms.

Sourahn

Sourahn, the eastern province, is a coastal region famous for its dramatic cliffs and vibrant cultural scene. Here, the arts flourish, with bards, performers, and artisans making their homes among the bustling port towns and cliffside villages. The province's economy thrives on trade, particularly in luxury goods and cultural exports. The people of Sourahn are known for their creativity and love of celebration, often hosting festivals that draw visitors from across Driochta.

Fovehr

Fovehr, the southernmost and most populous province, is the economic and military hub of Driochta. Dominated by the country’s largest city, Fovehr is a bustling center of trade, commerce, and political activity. The province’s flat grasslands are crisscrossed by trade routes, connecting it to the rest of the country. Fovehr’s militia is the backbone of Driochta’s defense, and its people are both industrious and resilient, proud of their province’s role as the nation’s powerhouse.

Tronjem

Tronjem, the western province, is a land of towering mountains and deep spiritual significance. The province is home to numerous temples and sacred sites dedicated to the gods, attracting pilgrims from all over Driochta. Nestled among the snowy peaks and ancient forests are small, secluded communities of monks, priests, and scholars who live in harmony with the rugged landscape. The people of Tronjem are devout and introspective, often viewed as keepers of ancient knowledge and tradition.
